Four games on the lawn. None of them take more than a minute to learn — pick one up and play.

Kubb

Sweden · 2–12 players

Knock over their blocks with thrown batons, then topple the king. Topple the king early and you lose on the spot.

How to play
  1. Five kubbs (blocks) stand on each team's baseline. The king stands in the middle.
  2. Take turns throwing your six batons underhand, end over end. No helicopter spin — that's a foul throw.
  3. Knock over the opposition's kubbs. They then throw those felled kubbs back into your half and stand them up as field kubbs.
  4. Field kubbs must be cleared before you may throw at their baseline kubbs. Hit a baseline kubb early and it doesn't count.
  5. Leave a field kubb standing and the other team may throw from level with it — much closer. Don't leave them standing.
  6. Clear every kubb, then knock the king with your next baton to win.

The one rule people break

Hitting the king while any kubb is still standing is an instant loss. The king goes last, always.

Golf Darts

Darts, but you chip · any number

Chip a golf ball at a giant dartboard. It sticks where it lands. That's your score.

How to play
  1. Chip from the mat at the board. Wherever the ball sticks is what you scored.
  2. It's a real dartboard: the outer ring doubles that number, the inner ring trebles it.
  3. Miss the board entirely and you score nothing. No re-throws.
  4. Five balls each, highest total wins. Tie? Nearest the bullseye takes it.

Feeling serious? Play proper 301 — start on 301 and subtract each shot, first to land exactly zero wins.

No video for this one — there isn't a good one, because there's no special ruleset. It's ordinary darts scoring with a wedge instead of an arrow. Chip gently; full swings send balls into the hedge.

Spin Ladder

Ladder Toss · 2–4 players

Two balls on a string. Throw them at a ladder and hope they wrap round a rung.

How to play
  1. Stand roughly five paces back from the ladder.
  2. Throw your three bolas (the ball-and-rope pairs) underarm, one at a time.
  3. A bola scores if it's still hanging on a rung once everyone has thrown — knocked off later by someone else's throw means it doesn't count.
  4. Bouncing off the ground first is completely legal, and often the best way in.
Top rung
3 points
Middle rung
2 points
Bottom rung
1 point

First to exactly 21. Go over and the whole round scores nothing — so late on, aim low deliberately.

Corn Hole

USA · 2 or 4 players

Bean bags at an angled board. In the hole is three, on the board is one.

How to play
  1. Two boards face each other about eight metres apart. Throw underarm, four bags each.
  2. Players alternate throws — you don't throw all four at once.
  3. A bag that lands on the board and slides in still counts as in the hole. A bag that touches the ground first scores nothing, even if it ends up on the board — take it off.
  4. Score the round, then throw back the other way.
In the hole
3 points
On the board
1 point
On the ground
0

Cancellation scoring: only the difference counts. 7 against 5 scores you 2, not 7. First to 21 — every crowd plays the bust rule differently, so agree on it before you start.
